## Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive **Fundamental Analysis of Tata Motors Limited** — one of India's largest and most globally recognised automotive manufacturers and a Fortune Global 500 company. The study applies the **top-down EIC (Economy–Industry–Company) framework** to evaluate the company's true investment worth.

The project begins with a broad examination of India's macroeconomic environment — including GDP growth, inflation, interest rates, FDI inflows, and trade balance — to understand the overall economic climate in which Tata Motors operates. It then narrows down to the Indian automobile industry, analysing the industry's life cycle stage, growth trajectory, competitive landscape, and structural factors. Finally, it deep-dives into Tata Motors' own financial performance — studying key metrics such as Earnings Per Share (EPS), Book Value, Market Value, P/E Ratio, P/BV Ratio, and calculating the stock's intrinsic value.

The ultimate goal of this study is to determine whether Tata Motors' stock is **overvalued, undervalued, or fairly priced** in the market — and to provide a clear, data-driven investment recommendation of Buy, Hold, or Sell.

Tata Motors is particularly interesting as a subject of study because of its unique dual identity — it is both a major domestic player in India's commercial and passenger vehicle markets, and a global premium brand through its **Jaguar Land Rover (JLR)** subsidiary. This makes its financial analysis more complex and insightful than a typical domestic company.

The study covers financial data from **2017–18 to 2020–21**, sourced entirely from Tata Motors' published Annual Reports, RBI monetary policy reports, BSE/NSE stock exchange data, and reputed financial databases.

**Key questions answered in this project:**
- What is the true intrinsic value of Tata Motors stock?
- Is the stock currently overvalued or undervalued?
- How has EPS, Book Value, and Market Value changed year on year?
- What macroeconomic and industry factors influence Tata Motors' performance?
- What investment decision — Buy, Hold, or Sell — should an investor make?
